Group Thoughts

As a group we believed our both our work quality and the deadlines were met with a lot more consistency than A2. When group mates were asked to complete their individual tasks they did so to a very high standard and were uploaded before the deadline. Further we all also agreed that communication for this assignment was good for the most part. The majority of group members attended meetings and reported our findings in the practical sessions as required. Having efficient communication allowed us to delegate and discuss tasks to put together the most coherent document we could. Things we could improve on in future assignments is the workload set for each team member. Everyone is studying different things and workloads at certain times may need to be a little different and flexible, we understood this from the beginning but some members failed to step up when required. Sharing the workload was difficult when people missed meetings as it became increasingly hard to delegate what was required. Although disappointing we were able to complete a good report in the end with wireframes and multiple tangible artifacts. One thing that surprised us was the genuine interest even began to have in the one common project. As the project developed and we could see things progressing the group became more invested in trying to create the most accurate and well designed product. We have learnt that even in the best of time mustering a group together can be extremely difficult and online learning has only made that more difficult. Further we have learnt that efficient communication is the key to success. When we were communicating well as a group the work required was being done to a high standard and very quickly, without this the work began to drop off and others had to pick up slack. The Github for our log only involves Radhika and this is a true reflection of our group's work. As Radhika is proficient in HTML we believed it would be best to leave all the programming work to one person so that it was as coherent and logical as possible. This was agreed upon at the beginning of the assignment and Radhika was required to do less of the content work to balance her load.
